I would be careful about presenting it like Hims.

In Australia, and particularly under AHPRA advertising guidelines, you generally cannot market prescription medicines directly to consumers in the way US platforms do. You can discuss treatment options in an educational context, but you cannot advertise specific prescription medicines as consumer products.

Instead, I'd position it like this:

Treatment Pathways
Evidence-Based Metabolic Medicine

Every treatment plan is individualised following assessment by a Meridian physician.

Treatment options may include:

Lifestyle & Behavioural Intervention

Nutrition, movement, sleep, stress management and habit formation.

GLP-1 Receptor Agonists

Where clinically appropriate and prescribed by a physician.

Dual Agonist Therapies

Including newer generation medications where approved and clinically indicated.

Emerging Metabolic Therapies

As evidence evolves and regulatory approvals occur.

Bariatric Surgery

For eligible patients seeking the most effective long-term intervention for obesity and metabolic disease.

Then on the physician-facing or educational section:

Current and Emerging Therapies
Class	Examples
GLP-1 Receptor Agonists	Semaglutide
Dual GIP / GLP-1 Agonists	Tirzepatide
Triple Hormone Agonists	Retatrutide*
Amylin Analogues	Cagrilintide*
Combination Therapies	Emerging

*Subject to regulatory approval and clinical availability.
