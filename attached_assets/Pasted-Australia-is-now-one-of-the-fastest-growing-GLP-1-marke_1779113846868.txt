Australia is now one of the fastest-growing GLP-1 markets globally, but the numbers also explain why bariatric and metabolic surgery remain extremely relevant.
Australia — 2026 obesity + GLP-1 snapshot
	•	~32% of Australian adults are classified as obese
	•	Another ~34% are overweight
	•	~13% are severely obese (BMI 35+)
That means roughly:
	•	~6.7–7 million Australians are obese
	•	~2.7–3 million Australians likely meet BMI 35+ criteria where bariatric surgery becomes highly relevant clinically
	•	~11–12 million Australians are overweight or obese combined
Australians using GLP-1 medications in 2026
Current estimates suggest:
	•	~500,000 Australians are using GLP-1 medications monthly (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro etc.)
	•	Health Minister Mark Butler stated ~400,000 Australians are currently paying full market price privately for GLP-1s
	•	UNSW-linked research estimated:
	◦	180,000–240,000 Australians privately accessing GLP-1s each month
	◦	~6 million units sold in a single year (2024–25)

Who qualifies for GLP-1s in Australia in 2026?
Typical prescribing criteria:
	•	BMI ≥30 OR
	•	BMI ≥27 with weight-related conditions:
	◦	Type 2 diabetes
	◦	Sleep apnoea
	◦	Hypertension
	◦	High cholesterol
	◦	PCOS
	◦	Cardiovascular disease
This potentially places:
	•	7–9 million Australians within “medical obesity treatment” eligibility territory.
Why bariatric surgery is still massively relevant
The market data actually strengthens the relevance of surgery.
1. GLP-1s are expanding the funnel
GLP-1s are:
	•	increasing awareness
	•	reducing stigma
	•	bringing patients into treatment earlier
But many patients:
	•	plateau
	•	stop due to cost
	•	regain weight
	•	remain severely obese
	•	require stronger metabolic intervention
That creates more long-term candidates for:
	•	sleeve gastrectomy
	•	gastric bypass
	•	revisional surgery
	•	multidisciplinary metabolic programs
2. Australia’s severe obesity cohort is enormous
If ~13% of adults are BMI 35+, Australia has:
	•	~2.7–3 million severe obesity patients
Yet only a small fraction undergo surgery annually.
Meaning:
	•	the addressable surgical/metabolic market remains extremely underpenetrated.
3. GLP-1 dropout is very high
2026 data suggests:
	•	~50% stop treatment within a year
	•	weight regain often occurs rapidly after cessation
This is driving a shift toward:
	•	combination care
	•	long-term metabolic pathways
	•	surgery + GLP-1 maintenance models
4. Melbourne is an unusually strong market
Melbourne has:
	•	affluent private healthcare uptake
	•	strong cosmetic/wellness culture
	•	high telehealth adoption
	•	high education around GLP-1s
	•	major private hospital infrastructure
	•	dense GP/referrer ecosystem
That makes it one of Australia’s strongest cities for:
	•	metabolic medicine clinics
	•	GLP-1 programs
	•	bariatric surgery
	•	multidisciplinary obesity care networks
The strategic shift in 2026 is becoming:
“GLP-1s are introducing patients to metabolic care. Surgery remains the highest-impact intervention for many of the most clinically complex cases.”
That is where the global obesity conversation is moving now.
