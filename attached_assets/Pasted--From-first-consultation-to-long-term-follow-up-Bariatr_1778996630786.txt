## From first consultation to long-term follow-up

Bariatric surgery is not simply an operation.
The first twelve months are considered part of the procedure itself — a structured period of recovery, adaptation and metabolic change.

For this reason, twelve months of planned follow-up are included within the surgical fee structure at Meridian.

Long-term follow-up beyond this period is strongly encouraged, and is associated with better nutritional outcomes, more durable weight maintenance and earlier identification of problems should they arise.

### 01

### Surgical consultation

A bariatric surgeon reviews medical history, weight trajectory, eating patterns, metabolic health and treatment goals.
Available surgical and medical pathways are discussed, with a written management plan provided.

### 02

### Dietitian assessment and investigations

Patients undergo baseline assessment including dietitian review, blood tests and body composition analysis.
Gastroscopy, sleep studies or additional investigations may be arranged where appropriate. Psychology assessment is recommended in selected patients.

### 03

### Pre-operative preparation

Preparation includes a two-week VLCD liver reduction diet, nurse education, pre-operative surgical review and anaesthetic assessment.

Patients receive detailed written information regarding surgery, recovery, supplements, eating progression and expected milestones after the procedure.

### 04

### Surgery and hospital stay

Procedures are performed laparoscopically within connected private hospitals experienced in bariatric and upper gastrointestinal surgery.

Most patients stay one to two nights. Early mobilisation, anti-nausea pathways and structured recovery protocols are used to support safer recovery and earlier return to function.

### 05

### Twelve months of included follow-up

Structured follow-up occurs with the surgical, nursing and dietetic team throughout the first year after surgery.

This period focuses on diet progression, behavioural adaptation, vitamin and nutritional monitoring, supplement optimisation and metabolic health review.

Patients also have access to the Meridian team between scheduled appointments where required.

### 06

### Long-term metabolic care

Many patients choose to continue long-term follow-up beyond the first year.

Ongoing review can help support durable weight maintenance, nutritional health, exercise progression and management of metabolic conditions over time.
