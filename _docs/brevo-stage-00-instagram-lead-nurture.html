A   Sequence Overview & Design Rationale

This sequence is a pre-consultation lead nurture for individuals who have reached MERIDIAN through Instagram (@meridiangimetabolic) or via a website subscribe form — but have not yet submitted a clinical enquiry or booked a consultation.

These are warm leads: they have demonstrated awareness of MERIDIAN and a degree of intent. The sequence is designed to build clinical credibility, reduce the uncertainty that delays first contact, and provide enough information about MERIDIAN's three care pathways that when the person is ready to take action, they understand what that looks like.

Tone principle: These communications should feel like a continuation of the MERIDIAN brand encounter — not a follow-up sales sequence. Clinically authoritative, calm, low cognitive load. Trust before conversion. The sequence earns the enquiry; it does not pressure it.

Structural inspiration — Everlab reference

The sequence structure draws on the Everlab Growth Engine funnel reviewed in May 2026, which demonstrated effective use of: (1) immediate email + SMS pairing; (2) a high-credibility "about the practice" email; (3) staged education across multiple touchpoints. Everlab's conversion tactics (urgency, "limited slots", direct sales pressure) are not used here — they conflict with AHPRA compliance and MERIDIAN's clinical brand positioning.

Entry triggers

Configure either or both of the following as entry triggers, depending on how contacts are created in Brevo from each source:

C   AHPRA Compliance Notes

These emails have been written to comply with AHPRA's Guidelines for Advertising Regulated Health Services. The following principles are applied throughout and must be preserved if copy is amended.
  • No testimonials or patient stories. No real or implied patient outcomes referenced.
  • No guaranteed or implied treatment outcomes. All statistics used are Australian epidemiological prevalence data (referenced in footnote where appropriate) — not outcome claims.
  • No urgency or scarcity tactics. Phrases such as "limited availability", "book now before spaces fill" or time-limited offers are absent and must not be added.
  • No before/after language. No implied comparison of patient state before and after treatment.
  • Factual clinical descriptions only. All procedure and service descriptions are factual summaries, not promotional claims.
  • No fear-based messaging. Statistics are presented as contextual prevalence data, not as risk or fear triggers.
  • No references to AI in copy. None of the copy references AI, algorithms or automated decision-making in patient care.

If any element of this copy is amended, the amended version should be reviewed for AHPRA compliance before the template is pushed to Brevo. Refer to the AHPRA Guidelines for advertising a regulated health service (updated 2020).

After Stage 00-05

The Stage 00 sequence ends after Email 00-05. No further automated emails are sent unless the contact takes an action that triggers a downstream automation:

  • If booked_consult is applied at any point during the sequence, the contact exits Stage 00 and enters the Booking Confirmation automation (Stages 06–07).
  • If the contact submits a formal clinical enquiry (via the website enquiry form, not the subscribe form), they may also enter the Enquiry Nurture automation (Stages 01–05). Coordinate entry conditions with the Brevo flow document to avoid duplicate sends in this scenario.
  • Contacts who complete Stage 00 without booking are retained in Brevo as warm leads and can be segmented for future campaigns via the source_instagram or source_website_subscribe tags.
Do not add further automated follow-ups beyond Stage 00-05 without clinical and marketing review. The sequence is intentionally bounded — a lead who has received five touchpoints and not yet booked should be managed through a separate re-engagement campaign, not extended automation.
