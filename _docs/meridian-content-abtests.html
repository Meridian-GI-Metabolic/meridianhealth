How to Use This Document

Each post in this bank was written with a single hook and a single caption. High-performing accounts learn faster by testing two or three opening lines per post, and by running both a short and a long caption variant, before settling on a house style. This document provides those alternatives for the ten posts with the highest expected conversion and engagement potential.

The Testing Framework

For each post, three alternative hooks are provided (Hook A, B, C). The original hook from the content bank is not duplicated here: assume it is Hook 0 and treat it as a baseline. Run Hook A in the first campaign cycle. Evaluate reach, save rate, and comment quality after 7 days. Rotate to the next variant in the following cycle.

For captions, two variants are provided per post: a Short form (under 100 words, optimised for Instagram engagement and mobile scroll) and a Long form (150 to 250 words, optimised for LinkedIn dwell time and saves). Run Short form on Instagram and Long form on LinkedIn by default in cycle one. Reverse the test in cycle two to see if audience behaviour surprises you.

GLP-1 vs Surgery: A Balanced View

Reel | Instagram + LinkedIn | Education and Evidence

Alternative Hooks (Frame 1 On-Screen Text)

Hook A

"GLP-1 medication changed metabolic care. For some patients, it is also not the right long-term answer. Here is the honest clinical view."

Hook B

"The question is not GLP-1 or surgery. The question is: what does this particular patient actually need?"

Hook C

"We are not anti-GLP-1. We are pro getting the right answer for the right patient. Those are different things."

Caption Variants

Short Caption (under 100 words | Instagram primary)

GLP-1 medications are genuinely useful. And for a significant proportion of patients, they are not the right long-term answer.

The question a specialist asks is: what does this person actually need?

That question requires a clinician who can see the full metabolic picture. Not an app. Not an algorithm.

We are happy to be that clinician. meridianhealth.com.au

Long Caption (150-250 words | LinkedIn primary)

There is a version of this conversation that is a polemic. We are not interested in that version.

GLP-1 medications represent a genuine clinical advance. For patients with moderate metabolic disease, no significant comorbidities, and a shorter duration of metabolic dysfunction, they are an appropriate, effective first-line option. The data supports this clearly.

The problem is the one-size application. When the same tool is offered to every patient regardless of severity, history, or individual metabolic architecture, outcomes diverge sharply from the clinical literature. Medication plateau, side-effect management, weight regain on cessation: these are not fringe outcomes. They are common ones.

What the Meridian model provides is the clinical capacity to assess every available option, without institutional bias toward any one of them. Michael is a specialist bariatric metabolic surgeon. He can recommend surgery. He can also recommend against it, and frequently does. That objectivity is only possible when the full range of interventions is genuinely on the table.

If you are on a GLP-1 programme and wondering whether you have the right long-term plan, a specialist conversation is worth having.

meridianhealth.com.au

Testing Notes

Run first: Hook B with Long Caption on LinkedIn. This post has unusually high share potential in a professional GP and clinician audience. The more clinical and intellectually precise the framing, the higher the expected dwell time and re-share from that segment.

Signal to watch: Comment tone and share rate. Comments that engage with the clinical argument (rather than questions about booking) indicate the post is reaching the GP referral segment. That is a valuable secondary conversion pathway and worth amplifying. Hook C will test better with a direct patient audience on Instagram: run it in cycle two alongside the Short Caption.

When we ask patients about the first weeks after surgery, they do not typically begin with what they see in the mirror. They describe what they feel.

Waking up without the heaviness that had become so familiar they had stopped noticing it. Making it through an afternoon without the crash that had become their normal. Climbing a flight of stairs without needing to pause. Sleeping through the night for the first time in years.

These are not cosmetic improvements. They are the direct result of metabolic recalibration. And they consistently appear before any significant change in body composition, in the weeks immediately following surgery, as the gut-brain axis and hormonal signalling begin to reset.

One of the most common things we hear from new patients is some version of "I had forgotten what normal felt like." Chronic metabolic disease erodes wellbeing so gradually that the erosion becomes invisible. Patients adapt to a diminished baseline and, in doing so, stop believing a better one is available to them.

The goal of metabolic surgery is not a number. It is a quality of life that many of our patients had given up expecting.

If you have stopped expecting to feel well, that expectation is worth revisiting. meridianhealth.com.au

Testing Notes

Run first: Hook A with Short Caption. The "not the mirror, what they feel" contrast is the most emotionally distinct opening in this set and directly challenges the assumption most patients carry into any metabolic care conversation. It will produce the fastest scroll-stop on Instagram.

Signal to watch: Save rate and comment depth. Posts that describe a physical sensation the audience recognises ("3pm crash", "couldn't sleep through the night") consistently produce high save rates because viewers are storing them to share with someone else. A save rate above 4% of reach is an indicator this piece has strong word-of-mouth potential and may warrant a paid boost. Hook B is more granular and concrete: test it in cycle two against an audience already familiar with the account.

Hook C

Caption Variants

Short Caption (under 100 words | Instagram primary)

Not all metabolic care models are built for the same patient. Swipe to understand the difference between subscription-model telehealth and specialist-led MDT care, and where Meridian sits within it.

Long Caption (150-250 words | LinkedIn primary)

The rapid expansion of GLP-1 telehealth has introduced millions of Australians to structured metabolic care for the first time. That is genuinely positive. Access matters. And the subscription model serves a meaningful patient segment well.

The question that follows from that expansion is the one that matters for more complex patients: at what point does the model reach its clinical ceiling? And what happens when it does?

Subscription-model telehealth is designed for scale. It is not designed for escalation. It does not include a specialist surgeon, a body composition suite, a psychology service, or a surgical pathway when non-surgical options have been exhausted.

Specialist MDT care, by definition, includes all of those things. It is also more expensive, more time-intensive, and more clinically rigorous than a monthly subscription. For many patients, that level of intervention is not what they need. For a specific and significant patient population, it is the only intervention that will produce durable outcomes.

The clinical conversation at Meridian always begins with the same question: what does this patient actually need? A specialist consultation does not commit you to surgery. It commits you to an honest answer.

Swipe to see the comparison. meridianhealth.com.au

Testing Notes

Run first: Hook B with Long Caption on LinkedIn and Hook A with Short Caption on Instagram simultaneously, since this post runs both platforms. Hook B names the price point directly, which creates a clear contrast and will generate faster engagement decisions from both audiences.

Signal to watch: Swipe-through rate on the carousel (aim for above 60% reaching slide 3) and the ratio of saves to likes. A high save-to-like ratio indicates the post is being retained for reference rather than passive appreciation, which signals higher downstream intent. Hook C is the most neutral and educational variant: run it in cycle two if Hook B produces negative sentiment comments about tone.

Static Post | Instagram | Competitive Positioning

Caption Variants

Short Caption (under 100 words | Instagram primary)

A $49 monthly metabolic subscription is a genuinely compelling offer. Easy access, medication delivery, basic guidance.

Here is what it does not include: a specialist surgeon. A surgical pathway. A body composition suite. Psychology support. A clinical escalation plan.

That gap is where Meridian exists. Not as a competitor to telehealth. As the specialist ceiling the right patient eventually needs.

When the subscription is no longer enough, we are here. meridianhealth.com.au

Long Caption (150-250 words | LinkedIn primary)

A low-cost GLP-1 subscription offers something real: easier access to medication, a digital care interface, and basic nutritional guidance. For many patients, particularly those with mild to moderate metabolic disease and no significant comorbidities, this is a clinically reasonable starting point.

The honest conversation is about what it does not include, and why that matters for a specific patient population.

It does not include a specialist surgeon who can assess whether surgery is indicated. It does not include a comprehensive body composition analysis, a psychological readiness evaluation, or a surgical escalation pathway when medication is insufficient.

It does not include long-term specialist-led follow-up, the kind that catches the early signal of a worsening comorbidity before it becomes a more serious problem.

These are not criticisms. They are category descriptions. A subscription-model telehealth platform was not built to deliver these things. It was built to scale. A specialist MDT was built to go deep.

For the patient who has been on a subscription for twelve months and is wondering why the results have plateaued, the next step is a specialist conversation. Not another dose adjustment.

When you are ready for the next level of care: meridianhealth.com.au

Testing Notes

Run first: Hook B with Short Caption on Instagram. The question "when the subscription stops working, what is the plan?" is the most direct articulation of the patient pain point this post targets. It is also the most likely to generate direct messages from patients at the plateau stage of a telehealth programme.

Signal to watch: Share rate. This is the post in the bank most likely to be shared by one patient to another who is on a subscription and questioning it. A share rate above 2% of reach justifies a paid audience expansion targeting users who have engaged with GLP-1 or weight loss content in the past 90 days.
