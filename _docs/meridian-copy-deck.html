Bariatric (Metabolic) Surgery

URL: meridianhealth.com.au/clinical-pathways/bariatric-surgery/

Hero

Bariatric (metabolic) surgery.

Lead: Personalised surgical options. Structured preparation. Long-term care.

Body: Surgical weight loss pathways managed by the same surgeon, dietitian and nurse.

Eyebrow: Follow-up suite  ·  Caption: Twelve months of structured review, in one place.

Who this pathway is for

When bariatric surgery may be appropriate.

Bariatric surgery may be considered when excess weight is contributing to long-term health risk and previous attempts at weight reduction have not produced durable results.

In general, this includes patients with a BMI above 35, or above 30 in the presence of weight-related medical conditions. Suitability is assessed individually within a comprehensive metabolic and surgical review.

This pathway commonly includes patients living with:

  • Type 2 diabetes
  • High blood pressure
  • Sleep apnoea
  • Fatty liver disease
  • Reflux associated with excess weight
  • Joint pain affecting mobility and daily activity
  • Repeated cycles of weight loss and regain

Procedures

Tailored procedures we have performed thousands of times. Choosing yours is anything but routine.

At Meridian, procedure selection is individualised around metabolic health, eating patterns, reflux, anatomy, previous surgery and long-term treatment goals. Surgical care is integrated with physician-led metabolic medicine, dietetics and structured follow-up.

Laparoscopic sleeve gastrectomy

The most commonly performed bariatric procedure worldwide. A significant portion of the stomach is removed to reduce hunger, improve satiety and support durable weight reduction.

Roux-en-Y gastric bypass

A well-established bariatric procedure combining restriction and selective malabsorption. Often considered in patients with significant reflux, type 2 diabetes or previous failed weight-loss interventions.

One anastomosis gastric bypass

A single-anastomosis bypass procedure performed in selected patients. Provides substantial metabolic and weight-loss effects with a streamlined surgical reconstruction.

Sleeve fundoplication

Combines sleeve gastrectomy with anti-reflux surgery. Considered in selected patients with obesity and coexisting reflux or GORD symptoms.

Gastric bypass with calibration ring

Gastric bypass with placement of a calibration ring to improve long-term restriction. Most commonly considered in selected revisional or higher-risk weight regain scenarios.

Gastric band removal

Removal of adjustable gastric bands for symptoms, intolerance, complications or inadequate weight control. Where appropriate, this may be combined with conversion to another bariatric procedure.

Revision bariatric surgery

Assessment and management of previous bariatric procedures, including weight regain, reflux, nutritional concerns, anatomical problems or procedural complications.

Revision surgery is often more complex than primary bariatric surgery and requires careful multidisciplinary assessment and long-term follow-up.

Image divider — Eyebrow: Recovery  ·  Caption 1: The first year after bariatric surgery is part of the treatment — not an optional extra.  ·  Caption 2: For this reason, Meridian includes twelve months of structured follow-up with the same surgeon, nurse and dietitian involved from the outset.  ·  Caption 3: Patients who remain engaged in longer-term follow-up often achieve more durable nutritional, metabolic and weight outcomes over time.

Process

From first consultation to long-term follow-up.

Bariatric surgery is not simply an operation. The first twelve months are considered part of the procedure itself — a structured period of recovery, adaptation and metabolic change.

For this reason, twelve months of planned follow-up are included within the surgical fee structure at Meridian. Long-term follow-up beyond this period is strongly encouraged, and is associated with better nutritional outcomes, more durable weight maintenance and earlier identification of problems should they arise.

01

Surgical consultation

A bariatric surgeon reviews medical history, weight trajectory, eating patterns, metabolic health and treatment goals. Available surgical and medical pathways are discussed, with a written management plan provided.

02

Dietitian assessment and investigations

Patients undergo baseline assessment including dietitian review, blood tests and body composition analysis. Gastroscopy, sleep studies or additional investigations may be arranged where appropriate. Psychology assessment is recommended in selected patients.

03

Pre-operative preparation

Preparation includes a two-week VLCD liver reduction diet, nurse education, pre-operative surgical review and anaesthetic assessment. Patients receive detailed written information regarding surgery, recovery, supplements, eating progression and expected milestones after the procedure.

04

Surgery and hospital stay

Procedures are performed laparoscopically within connected private hospitals experienced in bariatric and upper gastrointestinal surgery. Most patients stay one to two nights. Early mobilisation, anti-nausea pathways and structured recovery protocols are used to support safer recovery and earlier return to function.

05

Twelve months of included follow-up

Structured follow-up occurs with the surgical, nursing and dietetic team throughout the first year after surgery. This period focuses on diet progression, behavioural adaptation, vitamin and nutritional monitoring, supplement optimisation and metabolic health review. Patients also have access to the Meridian team between scheduled appointments where required.

06

Long-term metabolic care

Many patients choose to continue long-term follow-up beyond the first year. Ongoing review can help support durable weight maintenance, nutritional health, exercise progression and management of metabolic conditions over time.

Upper GI & Surgical Care

URL: meridianhealth.com.au/clinical-pathways/upper-gi-surgical-care/

Hero

Upper GI & Surgical Care.

Lead: Anti-reflux surgery, hiatus hernia repair, gallbladder surgery, hernia repair, and oesophageal and gastric cancer surgery.

Body: Performed by Upper GI surgeons within a single clinical team.

Eyebrow: Theatre  ·  Caption: Laparoscopic and open Upper GI surgery, in a single surgical service.

Who this pathway is for

This pathway is designed for patients requiring specialist upper gastrointestinal surgical assessment.

Patients are commonly referred for reflux, hiatus hernia, gallbladder disease, abdominal wall hernia or upper gastrointestinal cancer care and achalasia.

Referrals are welcomed from general practitioners and gastroenterologists. Self-referral is also appropriate.

This pathway commonly includes patients with:

  • Reflux or regurgitation not adequately controlled with medication
  • Confirmed hiatus hernia or para-oesophageal hernia
  • Symptomatic gallstones or gallbladder disease
  • Inguinal, umbilical or abdominal wall hernias
  • Oesophageal or gastric cancer
  • Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Barrett's oesophagus and complex foregut conditions

Procedures

Procedures are selected carefully against symptoms, imaging, physiology, prior treatment and long-term goals.

No operation is considered in isolation.

Anti-reflux surgery and hiatus hernia repair

Laparoscopic fundoplication and hiatus hernia repair for reflux, regurgitation and para-oesophageal hernia.

Various types of fundoplication are selected according to symptom profile, oesophageal motility testing and anatomical findings. Mesh reinforcement may be used in selected larger hernia repairs.

Heller myotomy

Minimally invasive treatment for achalasia and oesophageal motility disorders, typically combined with partial fundoplication.

Gallbladder surgery

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones, biliary colic, cholecystitis and gallbladder dysfunction.

Most procedures are performed as day-stay or overnight surgery.

Abdominal hernia surgery

Open and minimally invasive repair of inguinal, umbilical, incisional and abdominal wall hernias.

Approach is individualised according to anatomy, defect size, previous surgery and recovery goals.

Upper gastrointestinal cancer surgery

Gastrectomy and oesophagectomy for gastric and oesophageal malignancy, delivered within a multidisciplinary cancer pathway integrating surgery, oncology, gastroenterology, radiology and perioperative care.

Revisional foregut and complex surgery

Assessment and management of previous anti-reflux, hiatus hernia or upper gastrointestinal surgery, including recurrent reflux, recurrent hernia, dysphagia, mesh-related issues and failed prior procedures.

Endoscopy and diagnostic procedures

Gastroscopy and related upper gastrointestinal investigations performed as part of diagnostic assessment, surveillance and operative planning where appropriate.

Oesophageal physiology testing

Comprehensive oesophageal physiology assessment including pH monitoring and high-resolution manometry for reflux, regurgitation, chest pain, swallowing disorders and pre-operative evaluation.

Testing is interpreted within the context of symptoms, imaging and endoscopy to guide accurate diagnosis and individualised treatment planning.

Process

From first consultation through to recovery and follow-up, care remains coordinated through the same surgical team.

01

Surgical consultation

A surgeon reviews symptoms, prior history, imaging and existing investigations.

A clear management plan is discussed and documented during the consultation.

02

Targeted investigations

Where required, investigations may include gastroscopy, oesophageal manometry, pH monitoring, CT imaging or ultrasound.

Testing is selected carefully to clarify diagnosis and guide treatment planning — not simply ordered routinely.

03

Pre-operative preparation

Patients undergo pre-operative assessment with the nursing and anaesthetic team, with detailed written information provided prior to admission.

Preparation is tailored according to the procedure, underlying condition and overall health status.

04

Surgery and hospital stay

A minimally invasive laparoscopic approach is used wherever appropriate.

Most benign upper gastrointestinal procedures involve day-stay or short inpatient admission. Major cancer surgery may require longer admission with HDU or ICU support as part of a coordinated perioperative pathway.

05

Follow-up and ongoing care

Structured post-operative follow-up is provided to monitor recovery, swallowing, nutrition and long-term outcomes.

Patients undergoing cancer treatment remain within an integrated multidisciplinary oncology pathway involving surgery, oncology, gastroenterology, radiology and supportive care.

Clinical team

The same surgeon, from first conversation onward.

Care is delivered by the same surgical, nursing and allied health team.

  • Surgeon
  • Anaesthetist
  • Nurse
  • Dietitian
  • Physician where indicated

In practice

Most upper gastrointestinal conditions require a defined intervention. The role of assessment is to determine whether that intervention is necessary.

For many patients, this is a single procedure performed well. For others, the right decision is to hold off.

Metabolic Medicine

URL: meridianhealth.com.au/clinical-pathways/metabolic-medicine/

Hero

Metabolic Medicine.

Lead: Physician-led medical weight management, including GLP-1 therapy where appropriate.

Body: A medical pathway for patients seeking non-surgical weight management, pre-surgical preparation or treatment after weight regain.

Eyebrow: Consulting room  ·  Caption: Brenan Place, Fitzroy.

Who this pathway is for

For adults seeking medically supervised weight loss without surgery, or in the time before it.

GLP-1 medications such as semaglutide and tirzepatide are established therapies. Used carefully, with medical and dietetic supervision, they can produce meaningful weight reduction. Poorly monitored use creates avoidable risk.

This pathway includes patients with:

  • BMI above 27 with a weight-related condition
  • BMI above 30 without surgical intent
  • Difficulty maintaining lost weight
  • Interest in medical management before surgery
  • Previous bariatric procedure with weight regain

What is included

A programme designed around you, in six considered parts.

Physician consultation

Medical assessment, medication review and assessment of suitability for GLP-1 therapy.

Baseline investigations

Bloods, body composition scan and screening for comorbidities.

Dietitian-led plan

Protein-led nutrition, side-effect management and eating patterns that can be maintained.

Monthly check-ins

Dose adjustments, body composition review and side-effect support.

Muscle preservation

Resistance training and protein guidance to protect lean mass.

Surgical option held open

Direct access to a bariatric surgeon if surgery becomes appropriate.

Process

The same physician and dietitian, throughout. Treatment that responds to your numbers, not a script.

01

Initial enquiry

The enquiry is reviewed and directed to the appropriate physician.

02

Physician consultation

Full medical history, weight history, medication review and discussion of realistic expectations.

03

Bloods and body composition

Baseline blood tests, DEXA or InBody scan, and further investigations where required.

04

Treatment commencement

If GLP-1 therapy is appropriate, treatment begins with dose escalation, side-effect plan and dietitian support.

05

Monthly review

Same physician and dietitian throughout. Body composition is tracked. Treatment is adjusted to measured response.

Clinical team

Physician-led. With a surgeon on hand should you ever need one.

  • Physician
  • Dietitian
  • Nurse
  • Surgeon on request

In practice

GLP-1 therapy works better when monitored by a physician and dietitian over time.

Practice overview section

Upper GI surgery, bariatric surgery and metabolic medicine — integrated by design.

Meridian combines FRACS-accredited surgical care with physician-led metabolic medicine within one coordinated multidisciplinary practice.

The practice holds adjunct and honorary academic appointments with the University of Melbourne and Monash University. Patients are assessed, treated and followed up within the same clinical ecosystem, supported by continuity of care and a unified clinical record.

Eyebrow: Brenan Place  ·  Caption: Consulting suites, Fitzroy.

Standard of care

Specialist surgical and metabolic care, delivered with continuity, coordination and considered detail.

Experienced multidisciplinary team

FRACS-accredited Upper GI and Bariatric surgeons working alongside a physician-led metabolic team, dietitians and psychologists. The practice maintains adjunct and honorary academic appointments with the University of Melbourne and Monash University.

Contemporary evidence-based care

Current laparoscopic and endoscopic techniques. Medical therapies, including GLP-1 treatment where clinically appropriate. Ongoing involvement in surgical and metabolic research, education and quality improvement.

Coordinated patient care

A single clinical record across the practice. Assessment, treatment and follow-up managed within one multidisciplinary structure, with clinical review occurring at the time decisions are made.

Considered practice environment

Private consulting suites, calm interiors and time for unhurried consultation. The patient experience has been designed to support comfort, clarity and confidence throughout care.

Continuity throughout treatment

Patients meet their treating surgeon early and continue care with a familiar clinical team across surgery and follow-up wherever possible. Care remains coordinated within the practice rather than across disconnected providers.

Care between appointments

The Meridian patient app extends support beyond the consulting room with education, recovery guidance, progress tracking and structured follow-up tools reviewed by the clinical team.

Page header

Long-term metabolic care, under continuous clinical oversight.

Sub-lead: A physician-led membership for medical weight management and metabolic health.

Membership in Three Steps

Physician-led metabolic care, coordinated from the beginning.

Join through the Meridian app, complete assessment with a consultant physician, and remain under the care of the same multidisciplinary team across the year.

01

Download the app

Install the Meridian patient app on iPhone or Android.

Following first contact with the practice, your patient profile is activated to support appointments, communication and ongoing care throughout treatment.

02

Physician-led GLP-1 care

Where clinically appropriate, GLP-1 therapy is prescribed and supervised by a consultant physician within Meridian's metabolic programme.

Treatment selection, dose titration, monitoring and ongoing support are managed through the same clinical team to support continuity and safety.

03

Continuous care with the same clinical team

Your physician, dietitian and psychologist remain involved across assessment, treatment and follow-up throughout the year wherever possible.

Care is supported by scheduled review, GP communication and a shared longitudinal clinical record within the Meridian app.

Membership is opened on the recommendation of a Meridian physician. It is a clinical service, not a subscription product.

What it includes

Metabolic care rarely succeeds as a single episode of treatment.

Most patients require continuity, periodic review, adjustment of therapy and support that evolves over time.

Meridian Membership is designed to provide that ongoing clinical framework.

Membership is offered through recommendation by a Meridian physician or surgeon and provides coordinated long-term metabolic care within the broader Meridian model.

This is a clinical programme — not a subscription product.

Six elements of care

Six elements of care, held in one programme.

No. 01
Physician review

Quarterly consultation with a consultant physician. Adjustment of medication, review of bloods, escalation if needed.

No. 02
GLP-1 therapy

Where clinically appropriate, prescribed and titrated under physician supervision. Side-effect management included.

No. 03
Dietetic input

Sessions with an Accredited Practising Dietitian, scheduled to your phase of care.

No. 04
Psychology

Registered psychologist available for behavioural support, scheduled when clinically indicated.

No. 05
Patient app

The Meridian app, with reading, trackers, and direct messaging to your care team between visits.

No. 06
Shared care

Letters to your GP after every review. Clinical decisions remain visible and continuous.

Who it is for

Designed for patients who benefit from ongoing metabolic care rather than a single episode of treatment.

This pathway commonly includes patients undergoing long-term medical weight management, maintaining outcomes after bariatric surgery, or managing insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes and related metabolic conditions requiring periodic clinical review.

Membership is recommended selectively following assessment by a Meridian physician, ensuring the programme aligns appropriately with the patient's medical, metabolic and surgical needs.

A GP referral is helpful, but not required for initial enquiry. Medicare rebates may apply where eligible services are provided under a valid referral.

How it works

Three steps to enrolment.

01

Initial consultation

You meet a Meridian physician. The clinical question is examined in full, history, investigations and any current medication.

02

Pathway recommendation

If membership is the right pathway, the physician explains the programme, the medication plan, the review schedule and what is expected of you.

03

Twelve-month arc

Care unfolds across the year. Quarterly physician review. Dietetic and psychology input as required. The patient app holds the file in one place.

Diagnostics

Investigations held in the file, not chased between providers.

Periodic blood work, body composition, lipid and HbA1c monitoring, and other investigations clinically indicated for the medication and the patient. Pathology is requested through the practice and returns into the patient's clinical file.

Where additional imaging or specialist investigation is required, the practice arranges it within Brenan Place or with established referral partners.

Escalation

If the medical pathway reaches its limit, the surgical pathway is in the next room.

Some patients on the metabolic pathway will, over time, become candidates for bariatric surgery. The decision is reached through the same multidisciplinary review that opens the membership, and the surgical team is already known to the patient.

The transition is held within one practice. There is no second referral, no new file, no repetition of history.

Bariatric integration

The operation is only one part of long-term metabolic care.

Meridian Membership supports post-bariatric patients through ongoing physician review, dietetic care, supplement and nutritional monitoring, and management of weight regain or metabolic drift over time.

Care remains integrated with the operating surgeon and coordinated with the patient's GP to support continuity across the years following surgery.

Continuity

The same clinicians remain involved throughout the programme.

The physician who commences care continues review across the year, supported by a consistent dietitian and psychology team. Clinical records, progress and communication are maintained within a single longitudinal patient file through the Meridian app.

Overview

A quiet companion to your care. Not another health app.

The Meridian app holds the parts of treatment you would otherwise carry on paper, in your inbox, or in your head.

The four things you will use it for

No. 01
Today

Where you are in care, and the next step waiting for you.

No. 02
Library

Reading and worksheets written by Meridian clinicians, released as you progress.

No. 03
Trackers

Weight, hydration, protein and pain. Reviewed at each appointment.

No. 04
Messages

Reach your nurse, dietitian or surgeon between visits.

How it works

Three steps. We handle the rest.

01

Receive your patient number

At your first consultation, the practice issues a patient number. It links your app to your clinical file.

02

Your plan unfolds

The app releases content as you move through pre-surgical preparation, procedure week, and structured recovery. Nothing is shown out of order.

03

Your team reviews together

Trackers, questions and prepared notes appear in your file. Reviewed at each follow-up, not lost between visits.

Privacy

Your record stays inside the practice. Where it should.

The Meridian app is operated by Meridian Health. Patient data sits within the practice's clinical system. It is not sold, shared with advertisers or used for any purpose outside your care.

Login is tied to your patient number and a private code. The app can be paused or deleted at any time, with no effect on your clinical care.
