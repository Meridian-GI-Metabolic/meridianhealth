How to Use This Tracker

After each 7-day cycle, pull metrics for the post that ran and enter them in the relevant row below. After all three cycles are complete for a given post, review the numbers and record your declared winner at the bottom of that section. These records become the evidence base for your permanent house style decisions.

Pulling Metrics from Instagram Insights

  1. Open the Instagram app and go to your profile.
  2. Tap the post, then tap View Insights beneath it.
  3. Record Reach (unique accounts reached), Saves, and Shares from the Interactions panel.
  4. DMs: note any direct messages received in the 48 hours after publishing that reference the post topic. These are not shown in Insights and must be tracked manually.
  5. Enquiry spike: check whether a new enquiry form submission or consultation booking arrived within 48 hours of the post going live. Mark Y or N.

Pulling Metrics from LinkedIn Analytics

  1. Go to your LinkedIn Page or personal profile and open the post.
  2. Tap or click View analytics beneath the post.
  3. Record Impressions in the Reach column, Reposts in the Shares column, and any saves if your account type shows them.
  4. DMs: note direct messages received referencing the post topic within 48 hours. Track manually.
  5. Enquiry spike: same method as Instagram. Mark Y or N.

Column Definitions

Column What to Enter
Hook variant run Which hook was used: Hook 0 (original), Hook A, Hook B, or Hook C. See the Caption Variations document for the full text of each.
Caption length Short or Long. Short = under 100 words. Long = 150 to 250 words.
Platform Instagram or LinkedIn.
Reach Unique accounts reached (Instagram) or impressions (LinkedIn) after 7 days.
Saves Number of saves after 7 days. The single strongest signal of intent on Instagram.
Shares Shares or reposts after 7 days.
DMs Number of direct messages received referencing this post within 48 hours. Track manually.
Enquiry spike Y if a new enquiry or consultation booking arrived within 48 hours of this post. N if not.
Notes Any qualitative observations: comment tone, unusual follower spike, algorithm boost, or context that explains the numbers.
