Your first appointment

What to expect
when you visit.

Your first visit is a consultation. There is no commitment to surgery, and no decision will be made without you.

01

Sit down with a surgeon

You'll meet with one of our bariatric metabolic surgeons for a full clinical review — your history, your goals, your concerns.

02

Body composition scan

A quick, non-invasive scan that measures your muscle mass, body fat, and metabolic health — far more detailed than a BMI reading alone.

03

Nutritional review

You'll meet with a dietitian to talk through your eating patterns and what a pre- and post-operative nutrition plan might look like.

04

A clear path forward

The team will explain your options. If surgery is appropriate, you'll receive a written care plan. If it is not the right path, we'll tell you honestly and explore alternatives.

No referral required to enquire

You are welcome to call us directly. A GP referral is helpful but not required for an initial appointment.

Is this right for me?

You might be a
candidate if ...

  • ›

    You have type 2 diabetes and your blood sugar is still not well controlled, despite taking multiple medications.

  • ›

    Your weight has not responded to sustained diet and lifestyle changes over two or more years.

  • ›

    You have been on GLP-1 medication (such as Ozempic or Wegovy) and it has stopped working, or is unsustainable long-term.

  • ›

    Your doctor has told you that your weight is affecting your heart health, blood pressure, or other chronic conditions.

  • ›

    You have a BMI above 30 and significant metabolic health concerns, or a BMI above 35 regardless of other conditions.

You don't have to decide today

Coming to a consultation is just that. The team will assess your situation fully before any recommendation is made. Many people find the assessment itself clarifying, regardless of what comes next.

Your journey

What the process
looks like.

No surgical decision is made before the full team has reviewed your case and you have been fully informed.

01

Initial consultation

A thorough review with a surgeon. Your history, goals, and any concerns are discussed fully.

02

Body composition and investigations

A medical-grade scan and any required pathology or imaging to complete the clinical picture.

03

Nutrition and psychology review

Supportive assessment to understand your dietary habits and readiness for the pre- and post-operative process.

04

MDT review and recommendation

The full team reviews your case. If surgery is appropriate, a written care plan is prepared and discussed with you.

05

Surgery and ongoing care

Minimally invasive surgery, followed by twelve months of active follow-up. Your GP is kept informed throughout.
