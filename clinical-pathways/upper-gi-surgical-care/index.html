Pathway 02 · Surgical

Upper GI & Surgical Care.

Anti-reflux surgery, hiatus hernia repair, gallbladder surgery, hernia repair, and oesophageal and gastric cancer surgery.

Performed by Upper GI surgeons within a single clinical team.

Theatre

Laparoscopic and open Upper GI surgery, in a single surgical service.

Who this pathway is for

This pathway is designed for patients requiring specialist upper gastrointestinal surgical assessment, particularly where symptoms persist despite medical therapy or surgery has been recommended.

Patients are commonly referred for reflux, hiatus hernia, gallbladder disease, abdominal wall hernia or upper gastrointestinal cancer care and achalasia.

Referrals are welcomed from general practitioners and gastroenterologists. Self-referral is also appropriate.

This pathway commonly includes patients with:

  • Reflux or regurgitation not adequately controlled with medication
  • Confirmed hiatus hernia or para-oesophageal hernia
  • Symptomatic gallstones or gallbladder disease
  • Inguinal, umbilical or abdominal wall hernias
  • Oesophageal or gastric cancer
  • Difficulty swallowing (dysphagia)
  • Barrett's oesophagus and complex foregut conditions
Procedures

Procedures are selected carefully against symptoms, imaging, physiology, prior treatment and long-term goals.

No operation is considered in isolation.

Anti-reflux surgery and hiatus hernia repair

Laparoscopic fundoplication and hiatus hernia repair for reflux, regurgitation and para-oesophageal hernia.

Various types of fundoplication are selected according to symptom profile, oesophageal motility testing and anatomical findings. Mesh reinforcement may be used in selected larger hernia repairs.

Heller myotomy

Minimally invasive treatment for achalasia and oesophageal motility disorders, typically combined with partial fundoplication.

Gallbladder surgery

Laparoscopic cholecystectomy for symptomatic gallstones, biliary colic, cholecystitis and gallbladder dysfunction.

Most procedures are performed as day-stay or overnight surgery.

Abdominal hernia surgery

Open and minimally invasive repair of inguinal, umbilical, incisional and abdominal wall hernias.

Approach is individualised according to anatomy, defect size, previous surgery and recovery goals.

Upper gastrointestinal cancer surgery

Gastrectomy and oesophagectomy for gastric and oesophageal malignancy, delivered within a multidisciplinary cancer pathway integrating surgery, oncology, gastroenterology, radiology and perioperative care.

Revisional foregut and complex surgery

Assessment and management of previous anti-reflux, hiatus hernia or upper gastrointestinal surgery, including recurrent reflux, recurrent hernia, dysphagia, mesh-related issues and failed prior procedures.

Endoscopy and diagnostic procedures

Gastroscopy and related upper gastrointestinal investigations performed as part of diagnostic assessment, surveillance and operative planning where appropriate.

Process

From the first consultation through to follow-up. The same surgeon, throughout.

01

Consultation

A surgeon reviews symptoms, imaging and prior investigations. A plan is documented at the time of consultation.

02

Investigations

Gastroscopy, manometry, pH testing, CT or ultrasound where required. Only investigations necessary to make a surgical decision.

03

Preparation

Pre-operative assessment with nursing and anaesthesia. Written instructions provided prior to admission.

04

Surgery

Laparoscopic approach where appropriate. Day-stay to short admission for benign disease. Extended admission for cancer surgery with ICU or HDU support where required.

05

Follow-up

Structured post-operative review. Cancer patients remain within a coordinated oncology pathway.

Clinical team

The same surgeon, from first conversation onward.

Care is delivered by the same surgical, nursing and allied health team.

  • Surgeon
  • Anaesthetist
  • Nurse
  • Dietitian
  • Physician where indicated
Meet the clinicians
In practice

Most upper gastrointestinal conditions require a defined intervention. The role of assessment is to determine whether that intervention is necessary.

For many patients, this is a single procedure performed well. For others, the right decision is to hold off.

Considered care

From the first consultation through structured follow-up. Day-stay procedures, complex resections, and everything in between.

If you are unsure

Send your imaging across, and we will review it before your visit.

A clinician will advise whether further assessment or intervention is required.
