Australia ranks among the fastest-growing GLP-1 markets globally. Health Minister Mark Butler confirmed approximately 400,000 Australians are paying full private market price for GLP-1 medications, with UNSW-linked research estimating 180,000–240,000 privately accessing them monthly. An estimated 6 million units were dispensed in the 2024–25 year.

03 — GLP-1 Prescribing Criteria in Australia (2026)
Who Qualifies for GLP-1 Therapy
Primary criterion
BMI ≥30
Obesity diagnosis, no comorbidity required
This criterion alone places an estimated 6–7 million Australians within medical obesity treatment eligibility.
Secondary criterion (with comorbidity)
BMI ≥27
Overweight, with weight-related condition
  • Type 2 diabetes
  • Obstructive sleep apnoea
  • Hypertension
  • Hypercholesterolaemia
  • Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS)
  • Cardiovascular disease
Combined, these criteria potentially place 7–9 million Australians within the scope of medical obesity treatment. GLP-1 awareness has broadly expanded patient engagement with weight management pathways, increasing the number of adults seeking clinical assessment.
04 — Clinical Rationale
Why Bariatric Surgery Remains Clinically Relevant
1
GLP-1 medications are expanding the referral funnel
GLP-1 uptake has increased awareness of weight-related conditions, reduced stigma, and brought patients into treatment earlier. Many of those patients plateau, discontinue due to cost, or remain severely obese — and in time require stronger metabolic intervention. This creates a sustained pipeline for sleeve gastrectomy, gastric bypass, revisional surgery, and multidisciplinary metabolic programs.
2
Australia's severe obesity cohort remains largely untreated
With approximately 13% of Australian adults at BMI 35 or above — roughly 2.7–3 million people — the addressable surgical and metabolic treatment market remains substantially underpenetrated. Only a small fraction undergoes bariatric surgery annually, representing a significant unmet clinical need.
3
GLP-1 discontinuation rates are high
2026 data indicates approximately 50% of patients cease GLP-1 therapy within a year, with rapid weight regain commonly following cessation. This pattern is driving a clinical shift toward combination care models — long-term metabolic pathways that pair surgical intervention with GLP-1 maintenance protocols.
4
The field is shifting toward combination and continuity care
The emerging clinical consensus integrates GLP-1 medications within broader metabolic care models rather than positioning them as standalone treatments. Bariatric surgery carries a substantial evidence base for durable metabolic outcomes in complex cases — a position reinforced by documented GLP-1 treatment limitations in severe obesity.
05 — Melbourne Market Characteristics
Structural Advantages for Metabolic & Bariatric Care
Private healthcare culture
High rates of private health insurance and self-funded specialist care. Strong disposition toward specialist-led pathways.
Telehealth adoption
Among Australia's highest telehealth utilisation rates, supporting integrated metabolic care and GLP-1 monitoring models.
GP referrer density
A concentrated GP ecosystem across inner and middle Melbourne — well-positioned to generate consistent specialist referral volume.
Clinical infrastructure
Major private hospital infrastructure across the metropolitan area supports high procedural throughput and post-operative care continuity.
Health literacy
Above-average health literacy and GLP-1 awareness in the Melbourne population relative to national benchmarks.
Wellness engagement
High engagement with evidence-based health, nutrition and body composition — conducive to multidisciplinary metabolic models.
06 — Meridian Positioning
Clinical Context & Practice Position
Meridian operates at the intersection of upper gastrointestinal surgery, bariatric care, and metabolic medicine. As GLP-1 medications broaden patient entry into weight management pathways, the clinical need for specialist surgical and metabolic assessment has not diminished — it has expanded. Bariatric surgery carries a substantial evidence base for durable outcomes in severe obesity, and combination care models are increasingly the clinical standard for complex metabolic cases. Meridian is structured to address this cohort: patients who have engaged with medical treatments, require specialist assessment, and benefit from continuity of care across surgical, metabolic, and multidisciplinary pathways.
