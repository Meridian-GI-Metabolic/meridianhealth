Referral criteria

Who to refer

Consider a Meridian referral for patients presenting with any of the following clinical profiles.

Type 2 Diabetes with Medication Escalation

Long-standing T2DM with poor glycaemic control despite multiple agents; HbA1c persistently above 7.5%; patients who have not achieved remission through lifestyle or pharmacological intervention.

Elevated Metabolic and Cardiovascular Risk

BMI 35 or above with one or more metabolic comorbidities; BMI 30 to 35 with severe insulin resistance, cardiovascular risk, or metabolic syndrome. BMI alone is not the determinant of candidacy.

GLP-1 Plateau or Cessation

Patients whose GLP-1 pharmacotherapy has plateaued, is unsustainable long-term, or has been ceased with significant weight and metabolic recurrence.

Complex Obesity with Failed Conservative Management

Patients who have made sustained lifestyle effort over two or more years without durable metabolic benefit and are seeking a definitive, evidence-based intervention.

The evidence

T2DM remission rates in appropriately selected surgical patients exceed those of any pharmacological intervention at the five-year mark.

Long-term weight loss maintenance is superior to every non-surgical intervention at both the five and ten-year marks.

All-cause cardiovascular mortality is meaningfully reduced in surgical patients versus matched non-surgical controls.

MDT assessment

What every referred patient receives.

No surgical decision is made before the full multi-disciplinary team review is complete. The assessment is a clinical process, not a commitment to surgery.

The referring GP receives a written summary of all assessment findings and is invited to provide clinical context at any stage of the process.

01

Surgical Consultation

Specialist assessment by the bariatric metabolic surgeon, including full clinical history, surgical candidacy review, and discussion of procedure options where appropriate.

02

Seca Body Composition Analysis

Medical-grade measurement of skeletal muscle mass, visceral fat, bone mineral density, and metabolic phase angle. Results inform the surgical plan and post-operative monitoring targets.

03

Dietitian Review

Nutritional assessment and pre-operative optimisation by the MDT dietitian, with a protocol tailored to the patient's surgical pathway and existing metabolic profile.

04

Psychological Readiness Evaluation

Structured readiness assessment to confirm the patient's preparedness for the pre- and post-operative process, including lifestyle expectations and behavioural support needs.

05

Written Care Plan

A complete, patient-reviewed plan covering the surgical pathway, post-operative follow-up, and long-term monitoring schedule. A copy is shared with the referring GP and held in the patient's care record.

For the conversation with your patient

What to tell them before the referral.

›

This is a specialist consultation, not a commitment to surgery. The assessment determines candidacy; no surgical decision is made before the MDT review is complete.

›

Bariatric metabolic surgery is not a procedure of last resort. It is the most evidence-supported long-term intervention available for complex metabolic disease.

›

Meridian's care does not end at surgery. Patients remain under active team care through twelve-month follow-up and long-term monitoring, with the referring GP kept informed throughout.

›

The care team

Your patient's clinical team.

Every referred patient is managed by the same core MDT, with surgical, nursing, nutritional, and psychological expertise present throughout the assessment and post-operative pathway.

Michael, Bariatric Metabolic Surgeon

Fellowship-trained specialist surgeon with full professional accountability for surgical outcomes and long-term care. Leads the surgical candidacy review and the written plan shared with the referring GP.

Salena, MDT Dietitian

Pre- and post-operative nutritional assessment, optimisation, and ongoing dietary support throughout the care continuum. Provides a structured protocol aligned with the patient's surgical pathway.

Clinical coordination, pre-admission management, and the primary point of contact for patients between appointments. Ensures continuity of care and timely updates to the referring clinician.
